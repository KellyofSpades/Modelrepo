A Look at Two Presidents- Early 20th Century to Late 20th Century

William McKinley and Bill Clinton

Despite being the leader of the same country, the United States had major development during the 20th century.

William McKinley (1897-1901)

McKinley Analysis- State of the Union

"of the United States"

During McKinley's speeches, the use of the phrase "of the United States" is always mentioned. The phrase was used through mutiple speeches and the exact same in all six speeches analyzed.

McKinley Analysis- Inagurual Address

United States follow up

McKinley had the goal of making America up to date with many surrounding countries, and since this was almost the 20th century, America was still in development of becoming a booming country with the Industrial Revolution finishing the final touches in the late 1800s.

Overall Report

Summary

McKinley and Clinton's approach to speeches were always putting the countries "needs" into action rather than the countries "wants". For instance, McKinley made speeches saying how America would proceed to become more advanced during the 20th century, which at the time was seen more as a need since technology wasn't the greatest, so having access to large technological discoveries would show how well your country was doing. Clinton was president right after a war, so recovery from the war was critical for the United States.

These kinds of features in a speech are exactly what citizens wants to hear from a president, development to the country and any form of benefits they can get for living there. Whether or not the feature is actually carried out is up to the president in the long run, but promises to make a country better or recovered make presidents of different decades look the same.
