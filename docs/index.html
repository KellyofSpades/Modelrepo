Drawing Tips

I've been drawing for over 14 years and still struggle with some things when making certain drawings, but if you ever wanted to get started on how to get better at drawing, here's some little tips to keep in mind...

  • Practice makes perfect

As stupid or repetitive as this may sound, it's actually true. Take something you want to get good at drawing and try to draw it, now do it again, and again, eventually you'll get better at creating whatever it is you're doing to the point where it becomes muscle memory. You'll go from struggling to confident eventually, but only practice can help.

  • Try to reference over tracing

Tracing over another piece of art to add to your drawing is extremely easy to do, but what are you really learning from this? It's easier to look at how another person was able to draw something and follow along, plus you'll learn from trial and error over tracing and getting it right the first time, every time.

  • Positioning/Perspective matters

When drawing, remember that certain positions and perspectives change. If a character you're drawing is looking in a certain direction, keep in mind the parts facing the drawer are going to be much larger than the parts away from the drawer. If you're not really sure what this means, take a look at this image...

Notice how the left half is more obscured and smaller compared to the right half? Looking at the eyes can also help see this, the left eye is much smaller compared to the right, which is facing the drawer.

Characters

Throughout making comics, I've made a few characters to work with in the stories:

Agil Kilometer the Roadrunner

His name comes from the German word for "Agile" and Kilometer is a measurement of distance. Inspired from the Roadrunner from Looney Tunes, Agil is a confident, yet arrogant bird who's a master on the runways. Agil was originally meant to speak all in German and the amulet around his neck would change his vocals to English, but I changed it to just be decorative.

Astrape Mezocyklone the Cumulonimbus Cloud

His name comes from the Greek word for "Lightning" and Mesocyclone, a cyclonic air mass associated with supercell thunderstorms, or the storms that produce tornadoes. Inspired from my irrational fear of thunderstorms, Astrape is strict and demanding to always get his way, he's usually only seen around the person being afflicted and only they can see him. Astrape's gimmick works like a ghost.

Raynn Surgence the Shark

His name comes from a play on the word "Rain" and Surgence is a combination of "Surge" and "Vengeance". Raynn is a buff almost military-esque shark who's extremely loyal to his crew. He's the captain of a pirate ship. His crew would be different types of fish, Swordfish being the main fighters where they'd use their noses as the sword.

Sir Austeraus the Spartan

His name comes from the words "Austere", another word for "Spartan", and "Raus" the German word for "Out". Sir Austeraus is the strict and optimistic leader of the Lancelings, little knights with lances for noses.
